FYLE — BULK MILEAGE
EXPENSE REPORT

500 support tickets a month. One design insight — "common once, unique per row" — made them disappear.

Role

Fintech · SaaS
Platform

UX ResearchInteraction DesignFigmaUsability TestingFintech
01

Background

Fyle is an expense management platform. Field employees — sales reps, delivery agents — travel daily and log multiple trips at once. The existing flow forced one submission at a time. 15 trips = filling the same form 15 times with fields that literally never changed.

"Most users batch mileage submissions. Filling the same fields one at a time was causing real pain and real tickets."
— Fyle Product Team
02

The Problem

REPETITIVE FORMS

Same rate, project, cost center — every single trip. No batching. Pure tedium.

TIME-CONSUMING

10–15 trips = 15–25 min data entry. Finance backlogs everywhere.

NO DUPLICATION

Recurring routes re-entered manually every time. No clone option.

NO BATCH REVIEW

No running total shown. Users discovered errors only after submitting.

03

Research

01
500+ Ticket Analysis

Mileage = #1 complaint category. Repetition and time-loss dominated.

02
8 User Interviews

90% batched submissions weekly. Avg 12 trips per session.

03
Hotjar Session Recordings

Rage clicks on "Add Expense." High abandonment from screen 3 onwards.

04
Competitive Analysis

Expensify, Concur, Zoho — none had a streamlined bulk mileage flow.

04

Design Solution

Core insight: static fields (same across trips) vs dynamic fields (unique per trip). Fill what's common once. Fill what's unique per row. System calculates everything else.

📐Bulk Mileage Entry UI — Figma Prototype

STATIC MODAL

Rate, Project, Cost Center set once — applied to all rows.

BATCH TABLE

Date, Purpose, Route per row. Distance + Amount auto-calculate via Maps.

ROW DUPLICATION

One-click clone any row. Recurring routes covered instantly.

LIVE SUMMARY

Running total always visible. No more surprise errors before submit.

05

Process

01
Discovery

500+ tickets + 8 interviews + Hotjar + competitor teardown.

02
Mental Model Mapping

"Common first, per-trip after." Separation pattern from user mental model, not from existing UI.

03
3 Wireframe Directions

Inline form, modal wizard, spreadsheet table. Table won for 5+ entry scanability.

04
Hi-Fi Prototype

Static modal → batch table → row duplication → Maps autocomplete → live footer.

05
Usability Testing — 5 users

Row duplication: most celebrated feature. "This is why I wanted a desk job." Two mobile iteration rounds.

06
Dev Handoff

Annotated Figma: interactions, 20-row limit, empty states, offline fallback, breakpoints.

06

Impact

500+
Tickets eliminated Month 1
~70%
Submission time reduction
20×
Entries per session (↑ from 1)

Finance teams reported fewer backlogs. Row duplication became Fyle's most cited feature in reviews — called "game-changer" in 6 independent feedback responses.

07

Learnings

TICKETS = GOLD

500 tickets gave more clarity than any research sprint. Read them before opening Figma.

MENTAL MODELS WIN

Static/dynamic split emerged from user mental models, not from the existing UI.

MICRO → MACRO

2 hours designing row duplication became the most loved feature.

TEST IN REAL CONTEXT

Field workers test on phone in a parking lot. Desk testing misses the real UX entirely.

01

Background

Field service management platform — HVAC, IT support, elevator servicing. Brought in to go from zero to investor-ready MVP with no existing design, no system, no research. Brief: simple and terrifying.

Start with one user. Understand them completely. If it solves their problem, it solves it for the next ten.
02

One User Research

01
5 in-depth sessions

Full current workflow mapped — WhatsApp, Excel, phone, paper job cards.

02
Full-day observation

12 pain points found. 2 hours/day lost to manual status updates.

03
Tools audit

3 WhatsApp groups, 2 Google Sheets, 1 phone list, 1 physical job card binder. All of it was the product.

04
JTBD mapping

Each pain point reframed as a job. Became the MVP scope prioritisation framework.

03

Core Problems

INVISIBLE STATUS

No real-time job visibility. 40+ status calls per day just to answer "where is job 1042?"

MANUAL DISPATCH

3 sheets, no skill matching, no availability, no proximity view.

PAPER JOB CARDS

Physical cards got lost. Excel entries had errors. No history.

NO SERVICE HISTORY

Every job started fresh. No past context for customer or technician.

04

MVP Design

🗂️Hosakhsam — Service Board & Dispatch Panel
01
Service Request Board (Kanban)

All jobs by status — New → Assigned → In Progress → Done. Full day visible in 10 seconds.

02
Dispatch Panel

Inline assignment — load, proximity, skill tags. Dispatch: under 90 seconds.

03
Mobile Technician App

Two-tap status updates. Designed for gloves + outdoor light. Replaced paper entirely.

04
Service History

Timeline per customer. Ops manager called it "the most useful thing in the product."

05

Outcomes

Dispatch time (↓ from 8 min)
40+

3 investors and 2 pilot customers. All 5 independently cited the job board and mobile app as standout strengths.

DUSHU — CASE STUDY
University · 2022
University Project · Mobile App · Behavioral Design

DUSHU —
DAILY READING
HABIT APP

Existing apps tracked what you read. Dushu makes you actually read. 6/8 test users kept a 14-day streak.

Role
Solo Designer
Type
University Project
Platform
iOS Mobile App
Focus
Behavioral UX
01

The Problem

Most people who say they want to read more don't have a reading problem — they have a starting problem. Goodreads is a great library but a poor habit engineer. The brief: make picking up a book feel inevitable, not effortful.

Dushu (读书) means "to read" in Mandarin. Simple. Intentional. Just you and a book.
02

Key Design Decisions

STREAK (Loss Aversion)

Losing a 14-day streak hurts more than the reward of maintaining it. Loss aversion is more powerful than gamification.

FOCUSED READING TIMER

Distraction-free mode. Book cover, timer, silence. No sidebar. No feed. Just read.

MICRO-GOALS

"Read 10 pages today" beats "read more books." Small goals remove the start paralysis.

SOCIAL NUDGES

"Priya read a chapter." Not a feed. Just enough accountability without anxiety.

📱Dushu — Home Screen, Reading Timer & Streak View
03

Results

Tested with 8 participants over 2 weeks vs a control group using existing apps. 6/8 Dushu users maintained daily reading streak. Control group: 1/8. Streak loss aversion was cited by 5 participants as their primary motivation.

The biggest win was making breaking the streak feel worse than just reading 10 pages. Loss aversion > every reward system.
