FYLE — BULK MILEAGE
EXPENSE REPORT

How redesigning the bulk mileage submission flow reduced 500+ complaint tickets in a single month — by solving the #1 pain point for field employees at scale.

01 Context & Background

Fyle is an intelligent expense management platform used by thousands of businesses. Field employees — sales reps, delivery agents, healthcare workers — travel daily and need to log many trips at once. The existing experience forced one mileage expense submission at a time.

For a sales rep with 15 weekly trips, this meant filling the same form 15 times — including redundant fields like mileage rate, project, and cost center that never changed between entries.

"Our data suggests most users Fyle their mileage expenses within 1–2 days — meaning they batch submissions. Filling expenses one at a time with the same fields was a deeply painful experience."
— Fyle Product Team

02 The Problem

Support was receiving 500+ complaints per month specifically about mileage submission. Analysis surfaced four core failure points:

REPETITIVE FORMS

Every trip required filling all fields from scratch — even when rate, project, and cost center were identical. No way to batch common values.

TIME-CONSUMING

A field employee with 10–15 trips was spending 15–25 minutes on data entry alone — causing month-end backlogs for finance teams.

NO DUPLICATION

Recurring routes had to be manually re-entered each time. No way to duplicate a previous entry or set a recurring trip.

NO BATCH REVIEW

No summary of total distance and amount before submission — leading to frequent errors and resubmissions.

03 Research & Discovery

01
Support Ticket Analysis

Reviewed 500+ complaint tickets. Mileage was the #1 category — repetition and time-loss the top complaints by a wide margin.

02
User Interviews — 8 participants

Sales reps and delivery agents. Key insight: 90% batched submissions weekly with 5–20 trips to log at once.

03
Session Recording Review

Hotjar recordings showed rage clicks on "Add Expense" and high abandonment mid-session on multi-entry flows.

04
Competitive Analysis

Reviewed Expensify, Concur, and Zoho. None had a truly streamlined bulk mileage flow — a clear product opportunity.

04 Design Solution

The core insight was to separate static fields (common across all trips) from dynamic fields (unique per trip). This became the foundation of the Bulk Mileage redesign.

📐
Bulk Mileage Entry UI — Figma Prototype

STATIC FIELDS MODAL

Users set Mileage Rate, Project, and Cost Center once — applied to all entries. Removed the single biggest source of repetition.

BATCH ENTRY TABLE

Spreadsheet-style table. Fill Date, Purpose, and Route per row. Distance and Amount auto-calculate via Google Maps.

ROW DUPLICATION

One-click duplicate clones all values — perfect for recurring routes. Only date and purpose need changing.

LIVE SUMMARY FOOTER

Sticky footer shows running total of distance and amount as rows are filled — confidence before submission.

Fill what's common once. Fill what's unique per row. Let the system calculate everything else.

05 Process

01
Discovery & Ticket Analysis

Audited 500+ support tickets, interviewed 8 users, reviewed session recordings. Defined problem statement with product team.

02
Information Architecture

Mapped user mental model: "common stuff first, then per-trip details." Designed the static/dynamic separation pattern.

03
Wireframes & Concept Exploration

3 directions — inline form, modal wizard, spreadsheet table. The table won for scanability with 5+ entries.

04
High-Fidelity Prototype in Figma

Interactive prototype: static modal, table rows, duplicate interaction, Google Maps route input, live summary footer.

05
Usability Testing — 5 users

The duplicate row was the most-celebrated feature. Two rounds refined the mobile responsive layout.

06
Handoff & Dev Collaboration

Annotated Figma specs with interactions, edge cases (20-row limit, empty states), and responsive breakpoints.

06 Results & Impact

500+
Support tickets eliminated within Month 1
~70%
Reduction in average submission time
20×
Expenses submittable per session (up from 1)

Finance teams reported fewer month-end backlogs. The duplicate row became one of the most mentioned features in Fyle's user reviews — called a "game-changer" by field employees.

07 Learnings

TICKETS = GOLD

500 complaint tickets were more revealing than any survey. Quantified pain is the fastest path to design priority.

MENTAL MODEL MAPS

The static/dynamic separation only emerged from understanding how users mentally group their trip data.

MICRO = MACRO

One-click row duplication was a 2-hour design decision that became the most loved feature.

FIELD ≠ DESKTOP

Testing with actual field workers revealed mobile-first mattered — leading to a significantly improved mobile layout in v2.

01 Background & Brief

Hosakhsam is a service management platform for field service businesses — HVAC maintenance, IT field support, elevator servicing, facility management. I was brought in as lead designer to take the product from zero to a fully designed, investor-ready MVP with no existing design, no design system, and no prior user research.

Start with one user. Understand them completely. Then build for them — because if it works for your most important user, it'll work for the next ten.
— Design philosophy for the project

02 Starting with One User

01
5 in-depth interview sessions

3+ hours across multiple conversations. Mapped the current process — from WhatsApp messages to Excel sheets to phone calls.

02
Shadow & observation

Observed the user managing a full working day. Found 12 pain points in 8 hours of shadowing.

03
Current tools audit

Every tool used: WhatsApp groups, Google Sheets, phone calls, paper job cards. Time lost to manual updates: ~2 hours/day.

04
Jobs-to-be-Done mapping

Used JTBD framework to reframe each pain point. This became the backlog prioritisation framework for MVP scope.

03 Core Problems

INVISIBLE STATUS

No real-time visibility into technician location or job stage. All updates via calls or WhatsApp — easily lost.

MANUAL DISPATCH

Dispatching required cross-referencing multiple sheets. No skill matching, no availability check, no history.

PAPER JOB CARDS

Technicians carried physical job cards. Completed jobs manually entered into Excel at end of day. Errors and lost cards were frequent.

NO SERVICE HISTORY

No way to look up a customer's service history, previous technician, or recurring issue patterns.

04 MVP Scope & Solution

The MVP: minimum surface for one operations manager to run their day entirely within Hosakhsam — without falling back to WhatsApp or Excel.

🗂️
Hosakhsam Dashboard — Service Request & Dispatch Overview
01
Service Request Board

Kanban-style board — all open jobs by status (New → Assigned → In Progress → Completed). Assess the full day in 10 seconds.

02
Technician Dispatch Panel

Inline assignment — available technicians, current load, location proximity, skill tags. Dispatch time: under 90 seconds.

03
Mobile Technician App

Companion app to receive jobs, update status in real-time, capture sign-offs, attach photos. Replaces physical job cards.

04
Customer Service History

Timeline view per customer — all past jobs, technicians, notes, and resolutions. Built as read-only for MVP scope.

05 Process

01
Discovery: Single-User Deep Dive

5 interviews + 1 full day of shadowing. Created a 48-hour journey map across digital and physical touchpoints.

02
Information Architecture

IA for web dashboard (manager) and mobile app (technician). Card sorting with 20 users to validate navigation.

03
Low-fi Wireframes (Miro)

16 key screens. Iterated 3 times on the dispatch panel — the most complex and critical interaction in the MVP.

04
Design System Foundation

Lean system: 40 components, not 400. Built for speed and consistency over completeness at MVP stage.

05
High-Fidelity & Prototype

Full interactive prototype in Figma — web and mobile. ProtoPie for technician status-update micro-interactions.

06
Usability Testing

Original user + 2 additional operations managers. Key finding: offline mode needed for mobile — added to v1.1 roadmap.

06 Outcomes

12+
Core screens delivered for MVP
90s
Avg. dispatch time (down from 8 min)
40+
Design system components created
The MVP design was presented to 3 investors and 2 pilot customers. All 5 cited the job board clarity and technician mobile experience as the strongest aspects of the product.

07 Learnings

ONE USER ENOUGH

Deep immersion with a single user gave more clarity than any survey. Understand one person — patterns emerge naturally.

SYSTEMS SAVE TIME

Two days to build a lean design system paid off massively — made the last 60% of screens 3× faster to design.

CONTEXT = CONSTRAINT

Field technicians work in conditions most UX designers never consider — sunlight, gloves, time pressure.

HARDEST FLOW FIRST

We iterated the dispatch panel 3 times before the first usability test. Front-load effort on the most complex interaction.
